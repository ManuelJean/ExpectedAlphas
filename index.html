About

A publication
built on
curiosity.

The Examined Mind is an intellectual publication dedicated to long-form essays, research articles, and careful analysis of ideas across economics, mathematics, computer science, and philosophy.

This is not a blog in the casual sense. Each piece is written with the intention of genuinely advancing understanding—of following an idea wherever it leads, even when that destination is unexpected or uncomfortable. The writing here is a record of thinking out loud.

The goal is a kind of interdisciplinary synthesis: to bring together insights from different fields and test them against each other. To ask what economics can teach us about human nature, what mathematics reveals about the structure of certainty, what computer science tells us about intelligence itself.

Readers here tend to be students, academics, researchers, and anyone who believes that careful thinking is among the highest human activities. Every essay is written for a reader who is willing to slow down.
